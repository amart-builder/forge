# Forge: CRM + Email Handler + Morning Brief — Product Spec & Build Plan

## North Star

Every person Alex sets up with OpenClaw on a Mac Mini immediately gets a working CRM that:
1. Their AI agent walks them through connecting their email and importing existing contacts
2. Automatically starts running — handling all their emails, contacts, and relationships
3. Includes a daily morning brief that tells them what to focus on

This is the core product experience that justifies the $5,000 setup fee. The Mac Mini setup alone is utility; Forge is what makes customers say "holy shit, this is worth it."

## Feature 1: Forge (CRM + Email Handler)

### 1.1 Setup Flow

When a customer's OpenClaw is set up, Navi should have a skill or command that walks them through connecting their email and getting Forge running. The flow:

1. Customer says "set up Forge" or Navi proactively offers during onboarding interview
2. Navi walks them through:
   - Connecting Gmail or Outlook (OAuth via `gog auth`)
   - Connecting Google Calendar (`gog cal` auth)
   - Connecting Google Drive (`gog drive` auth)
   - Importing existing contacts from Google Contacts (`gog contacts`) or a CSV export from their current CRM
3. Navi creates the Supabase tables (or local SQLite DB) for CRM data
4. Navi sets up the email triage cron job
5. Navi opens the Forge web app and confirms everything works

### 1.2 The Forge App

A web app that runs locally on the Mac Mini. The customer accesses it via a URL (e.g., `http://localhost:PORT` or a Tailscale URL from their laptop).

**Two tabs:**

#### Tab 1: Email Handler

**Top section — Summary card:**
- "Here's what happened since you last looked"
- Count of new emails processed, actions taken, items needing attention
- Short natural-language summary of what happened (generated by Navi using Opus reasoning)

**Middle section — Action cards:**
- Each email that needs attention is a card
- Card shows: sender name/photo, subject, context summary (why this matters), relationship context from CRM
- AI-recommended action: Reply / Archive / Follow Up / Delegate / Flag
- For replies: full suggested response text (editable inline), one-click "Send" button
- For other actions: one-click execute button
- Cards sorted by priority (AI-determined)

**Bottom section — Action log:**
- Everything the email cron did since last check
- Auto-archived emails, auto-classified items, follow-up reminders set
- Audit trail so user can always verify nothing important was missed

**Goal: The user should never need to open Gmail again.**

#### Tab 2: CRM (DenchClaw-inspired)

- **Contact list** — searchable, filterable by tag/company/relationship tier
- **Contact detail view:**
  - Name, company, role, email, phone
  - How you know them (relationship context)
  - Last touchpoint (auto-updated from email activity)
  - Relationship tier (configurable)
  - All conversation history (email threads, meeting notes)
  - Notes (manual + AI-generated)
  - Tags
- **Auto-add:** New contacts from email are automatically added to the CRM
- **Meeting notes pipeline:** Meeting notes/summaries received via email are auto-detected and appended to the relevant contact's file. If the contact doesn't exist, they're auto-created.
- **Import:** Bulk import from Google Contacts, CSV, or other CRM exports

### 1.3 Email Triage Engine (adapted from Alex's system)

**Schedule:** 3x/day during work hours on weekdays (e.g., 9am, 1pm, 5pm — configurable per user's timezone)

**Classification (adapted from Alex's 3-tier system for generic users):**
- **Tier 0 — Auto-archive:** Spam, marketing, newsletters (unless user follows), 2FA codes, automated notifications
- **Tier 1 — Notify only:** FYI emails, CC'd threads, informational updates. Logged in action log but no action card.
- **Tier 2 — Needs action:** Direct emails requiring a response or decision. Full context assembly → draft response → action card in Forge.

**Context assembly for each Tier 2 email:**
1. Read full email thread (not just latest message)
2. Check sent mail — has user already responded?
3. Pull CRM data for sender — who are they, relationship history, last interaction
4. Check calendar — any upcoming meetings with this person?
5. Review past corrections — has the user corrected Navi's drafts for this person before? Learn from those.

**Draft generation:**
- Use Opus reasoning to generate a suggested reply
- Consider: sender identity, relationship context, email tone, thread history, user's communication style
- Match user's voice (learned from their sent emails over time)
- Never auto-send — always present for approval/editing

**Follow-up tracking:** Emails sent 3+ business days ago with no reply → "follow up needed" card
**Stale cleanup:** Pending action items older than 7 days → auto-dismiss with log entry

### 1.4 Meeting Notes Pipeline

- Detect meeting notes/summaries in incoming emails (common formats: Google Meet transcripts, Zoom summaries, Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, manual notes)
- Parse out: attendees, date, key points, action items
- Match attendees to CRM contacts
- Append meeting summary to each contact's record
- If a contact doesn't exist in CRM → auto-create with available info
- Surface action items in the next morning brief

## Feature 2: Daily Morning Brief

### 2.1 Delivery

- Delivered via Discord (primary channel) as a formatted message
- Also visible in the Forge app as a pinned notification
- Configurable delivery time based on user's timezone and preference (default: 8am local)

### 2.2 Schedule

- OpenClaw cron job, runs daily on weekdays
- Skips weekends by default (configurable)
- Runs as an isolated agent session with full reasoning

### 2.3 Content

The morning brief should be AI-reasoned, not templated. Navi uses Opus to decide what's important to surface. But it should cover:

1. **Email status** — "You have X emails needing attention in Forge" with the top 1-2 most important highlighted
2. **Calendar** — Today's schedule + anything notable tomorrow. Flag conflicts or back-to-backs.
3. **Goals & progress** — Based on GOALS.md, what has the user been working toward? Any stalled goals?
4. **Recent activity recap** — What has the user been doing the past few days? (From memory/conversation history)
5. **AI recommendations** — How should they spend today? What's the highest priority? What should they action first?
6. **Action items** — Any overdue follow-ups, stale tasks, or things that fell through the cracks
7. **Forge CRM nudges** — Contacts they haven't talked to in a while who might be worth reaching out to

### 2.4 Reasoning Approach

Heavy reliance on the agent's reasoning. The brief should NOT be a template with filled-in blanks. It should be a thoughtful assessment that sounds like a sharp chief of staff gave a verbal download. The AI should:
- Prioritize ruthlessly (don't list everything — highlight what matters)
- Make actual recommendations ("I'd focus on X today because...")
- Flag things the user might have missed
- Be concise — this should take 2-3 minutes to read, not 10

### 2.5 Personalization Over Time

- As Navi learns the user's patterns (via Mem0 + conversation history), the brief gets more targeted
- Learn which types of items the user cares about vs dismisses
- Learn their work rhythm (when they're most productive, what they tend to procrastinate on)
- Learn their communication preferences (do they want detail or bullets?)
